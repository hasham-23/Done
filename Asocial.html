Disaster and crisis relief

In the wake of natural disasters and crisis situations worldwide,
ZaraTherapy shares resources and provides impacted communities
and individuals with free mental health care and support.

Following the Highland Park shooting,ZaraTherapy reached out to help with free therapy for survivors and those impacted.

During the humanitarian crisis in Ukraine, ZaraTherapy was there for Ukrainians worldwide by providing free therapy to those impacted.

In the aftermath of the shooting at Robb Elementary School in Uvalde, TX, ZaraTherapy reached out to provide free therapy to families impacted.

In response to Hurricane Ida, ZaraTherapy donated therapy to individuals and communities affected.

ZaraTherapy supported the LGBTQ+ community and individuals impacted by the shooting at Club Q in Colorado Springs with free therapy.

To help families impacted by the horrific flooding that hit the Midwest in the Summer of 2022,ZaraTherapy provided free therapy.

In response to the shooting at the Central Visual and Performing Arts High School in St.Louis, ZaraTherapy provided support through therapy to those in need.

Partners and community

To further our mission, ZaraTherapy partners with over 100 impactful non-
profit organizations, to donate free therapy to a variety of under-
resourced communities and break down barriers to accessing therapy for
people in need.




The Future of Mental Health Scholarship

As part of our mission to make high-quality mental health care accessible and affordable
for anyone in need, ZaraTherapy is investing in the future of mental healthcare providers
through our scholarship program: the Future of Mental Health Scholarship.


This program calls upon emerging scholars in this space to share their stories and passion
for mental health in order for us to support and empower their impact on the field.


Our fund currently awards 10 college students studying psychology or related fields with
$10,000 towards their education.
